TOA Physical Damage & Non-Trucking Liability Programs

1. Introduction & Key Parties

All United Risk Managers LLC is the claims administration company designated by the Truck Owners Association (TOA). When a covered driver has an accident or loss, we are the people they contact.

The Key Parties

PartyRole
TOANamed Insured. Creates and administers the Benefits-Package Programs. Enrolls members, approves drivers, issues certificates.
Agents House Inc.TOA's designated programs administrator. Issues certificates on TOA's behalf.
Underwriters / Insurance Co.Surplus lines insurance company that issues the underlying policy.
All United Risk ManagersClaims administrator. Processes claims, coordinates adjusters, handles claims lifecycle.
Coverage HolderTruck owner/company that enrolls vehicles and drivers. NOT the Named Insured — a beneficiary only.
Approved DriverPerson approved by TOA to operate enrolled vehicles, documented by a certificate.
Agents / BrokersSell TOA programs. Do NOT have authority to issue certificates or change terms.
Critical Concept: The coverage holder is NOT the insured. TOA is the Named Insured. The coverage holder is solely a beneficiary. Only the vehicles/equipment are insured, not the person.

3. How TOA Programs Work

The Contract Process

  1. Application — Sponsor submits a Quote/Application (this IS the offer)
  2. Acceptance — TOA can accept only by issuing a Certificate. No contract until then.
  3. Binding — T&C, Quote/App, Certificate, and policies become the Contract.
  4. MVRs Required — Motor vehicle records required to bind any benefit.

Key Rules

  • Active TOA membership required for ANY benefit
  • Coverage is continuous until cancelled — no expiration date
  • Only certificates from TOA / Agents House are valid
  • Agent/broker certificates have NO force or effect

5. NTL Benefits-Package Program

PD = damage to YOUR enrolled auto  |  NTL = liability for damage/injury to OTHERS while using your truck for personal (non-trucking) purposes

Coverage territory: US, territories/possessions, Puerto Rico. (Note: PD includes Canada; NTL does not.)

Two Enrollment Options

OptionDetails
Long-Term LeaseMust lease auto to a regulated Motor Carrier within 48 hours. Provide signed lease within 48 hours. Auto-cancels if not met. Expires when lease ends.
Short-Term (20-Day)Enrollment for exactly 20 days. Cannot cancel early. Auto-expires.

Important NTL Rules

  • Supplementary to commercial insurance
  • Household members EXCLUDED
  • NO coverage while used for business or transporting cargo

6. The Five Coverage Requirements — PD

If ANY one requirement is not satisfied, the claim is DENIED.
1
Auto Must Be Enrolled
Specifically described in the PD Certificate, shown as enrolled at time of loss.
2
All Drivers Must Be Approved
ALL persons inside must be active TOA members with certificates issued BEFORE entering the auto.
3
All Drivers Must Be "Acceptable"
MVR must meet criteria. Applies EVEN IF driver was approved and certificated.
4
Commercial Purpose
If driver is using the auto at time of loss, it must be for a commercial purpose.
5
Full Compliance
All T&C, Policy, Quote/Application, and Certificate must be complied with.

Critical Points

  • Unenrolled drivers cannot be given permission — auto is ineligible
  • Stolen autos are ineligible even if enrolled unknowingly
  • Tractor and trailer are TWO separate autos — each needs its own certificate
  • Failure = loss of ALL benefits (Legal, Claims, Deductible Waiver, etc.)

7. The Five Coverage Requirements — NTL

1
Auto Must Be Enrolled in NTL Certificate at time of bodily injury or property damage.
2
All Drivers Approved with certificates issued BEFORE entering auto.
3
All Drivers Acceptable — same MVR criteria as PD.
4
NOT Used for Business or Cargo — must be non-business, non-commercial.
5
Full Compliance with all T&C.

"In the Business of Any Lessee" Includes:

  • For the benefit of or furthering the interest of any lessee
  • Acting within scope of employment by any lessee
  • Under direction, control, or dispatch of any lessee
  • Traveling to/from locations for cargo pickup/delivery
  • Traveling between garage and terminal/facility for cargo purposes

9. Violation Categories

Critical Violations

ANY in last 5 years = Unacceptable

  • DWI / DUI (alcohol)
  • Any alcohol-related driving violation
  • DUI (drugs / controlled substances)
  • Any drug-related driving violation
  • License suspension for failure to submit to testing

Major Violations

ANY in last 5 years = Unacceptable

  • Manslaughter / reckless homicide
  • Felony involving a motor vehicle
  • Racing
  • Hit and run
  • Reckless driving
  • License suspension for points
  • Driving while suspended/revoked
  • Fleeing/eluding a peace officer
  • Driving over 100 mph / 160 km/h
  • Driving CMV without required CDL

Minor Violations

Limits: 5 in 5 yrs / 3 in 3 yrs

  • Any moving violation not critical/major
  • Defective brakes
  • Defective equipment
  • Oversize / overweight vehicle
Violations count whether or not they are actually recorded on the MVR — the test is whether they "would be recorded."

11. Duties in the Event of Loss

1
Call the Police
Required if damage > $5,000, a crime is involved, or there was bodily injury / third-party property damage. Driver must WAIT and give an account.
2
Report to All United
Within 24 hours. Online, email, phone, or fax.
3
Photograph / Video Everything
Scene, damage, cause — IMMEDIATELY at the scene. Must have working camera at all times.

Required Documentation

Photos / video Driver's signed statement CDL copy MVR (< 30 days old) Driver's phone number Employment application / lease Log book (8 days) Police / fire report Damage estimate & towing bill Registration & title Liens & loss payees

Missing Documents Timeline

First Notice2nd calendar day after claim filed
Second Notice3rd day after First Notice
Third & Final Notice5th day after Second Notice — 6-day deadline stated
Claim Suspended6 days after Third Notice if docs still missing

Reopen deadline: 12 months from date of loss.

Other Critical Duties

  • Do not impair recovery rights or release claims against others
  • Send legal papers immediately
  • Allow inspection before repairs (failure = claim invalidated)
  • Cooperate fully & produce drivers for interviews (failure = claim invalidated)
  • Provide interpreter if driver doesn't speak English (at coverage holder's expense)
  • Submit Proof of Loss within 60 days

12. Deductibles & Deductible Waiver

$20,000
Base Policy Deductible (per unit)
$1,000
Lowest Possible w/ Waiver

The Deductible Waiver Benefit means TOA covers an agreed portion of the $20,000. The certificate shows the "combined deductible" (e.g., $20,000 − $19,000 waiver = $1,000).

You LOSE the Waiver If:

  • The five coverage requirements are NOT satisfied
  • A T&C violation makes the loss ineligible

Result: coverage holder pays the full $20,000.

Special Deductible Situations

Tractor and trailer each have their own separate deductible.

13. Limits of Liability & Valuation

Maximum payout = the LESSER of:

  1. Stated value on the Certificate
  2. Actual Cash Value (ACV) at time of loss
  3. Cost of repair / rebuild
  4. Policy limit ($175,000/unit; $1,500,000/occurrence)

MINUS the deductible.

Understated Value Penalty

If stated value < ACV by the greater of $7,500 or 10% of stated value, payout is proportionally reduced.

ACV Rules

  • Based on ORIGINAL factory-installed options only
  • Aftermarket items NOT considered
  • Prior damage deducted
  • Depreciation: 12.5% per year

Rebuilt Title Multipliers

DisclosureMultiplier
Written disclosure made70% of normal limits
No disclosure50% of normal limits

16. Mandatory Endorsements

These apply to ALL PD Program enrollees. Non-compliance can void coverage.

1. Functional Camera

Driver must ALWAYS have a fully-charged, functional camera on their person or within immediate reach. Failure = no duty to provide any benefit or payment.

2. Approved Driver

ALL persons inside must be approved drivers with valid certificates issued BEFORE entering the vehicle. No exceptions.

3. Police Report

If damage >$5K or crime involved: call police, wait, cooperate, submit report. Crime + no compliance = VOID. Non-crime + no compliance = limit reduced to $5K or 25% ACV. Exceptions: incapacity, police arrived on own, non-arson fire, police refuse to come (follow alternate steps).

4. Right of First Denial

TOA can deny a claim first; Underwriters can overrule and pay without it counting against TOA's aggregate deductible.

5. OFAC Search & Identity Investigation

Claimants/drivers may be asked for proof of citizenship/residency. Failure to provide = claim invalidated.

6. Depreciation

In ACV disagreements: 12.5% per year depreciation may be applied to purchase price or stated value.

7. Disclosure of Past Claims

New applicants must disclose ALL claims/accidents/losses from last 3 years. Failure = coverage VOID for open or future claims.

8. Rebuilt Title

Must disclose in writing. Disclosed: 70% multiplier. Not disclosed: 50% multiplier.

9. Conestoga & Non-Factory Equipment

Must be permanently attached and reported at binding. Conestoga: max $15,000 with $2,500 deductible. Other: max $1,000. Depreciation: 12.5%/year.

19. Fraud, Misrepresentation & Concealment

What Constitutes Fraud

  • Concealing or misrepresenting material facts
  • Fraud, attempted fraud, or false oath
  • Threats related to a claim
  • Applying under a new name/entity to hide claims history
  • Concealing driver violations or accidents
  • Providing false identity documents

Consequences

  • Coverage VOID for open or future claims
  • May be cancelled from inception (as if never in effect)
  • Premium may be increased from inception with immediate shortfall
  • Applies to coverage holder AND their employees, friends, family, agents
